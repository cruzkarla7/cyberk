# Conflict of Interest Policy  

## Introduction  
This Conflict of Interest Policy is designed to help employees, board members, and affiliated individuals of the organization identify situations that present potential conflicts of interest and to provide guidance on how to handle such situations.  

## Definition of Conflict of Interest  
A conflict of interest occurs when an individual's personal interests - financial, familial, or otherwise - interfere with their ability to act in the best interests of the organization.  

## Guidelines  
1. **Disclosure**:  
   - Employees and associated individuals must disclose any potential conflicts of interest to their immediate supervisor or the human resources department.  
   - Disclosure should occur at the onset of employment and any time a potential conflict arises thereafter.  

2. **Accepting Gifts or Benefits**:  
   - Employees may not accept gifts, donations, or benefits from individuals or organizations that have business dealings with the organization without prior approval.  

3. **Outside Employment**:  
   - Employees must obtain written approval from management before engaging in outside work that may create a conflict of interest.  

4. **Family Relationships**:  
   - Any employee involved in the hiring or management of family members must disclose this relationship to avoid conflicts.  

5. **Review and Management**:  
   - Management will review disclosed conflicts of interest and determine the appropriate course of action. This may include remediation strategies or, in severe cases, termination of employment.  

## Conclusion  
Transparency and accountability are crucial in upholding the integrity of our organization. All employees and affiliated individuals are expected to abide by this Conflict of Interest Policy to ensure ethical decision-making and maintain trust both within and outside the organization.
