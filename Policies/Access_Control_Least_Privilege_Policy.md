# Access Control and Least Privilege Policy

## Introduction
This document outlines the access control and least privilege policy for the organization. The principle of least privilege mandates that users should have only the minimum level of access necessary to perform their job functions.

## Policy Statement
The organization is committed to ensuring that access to sensitive data and systems is granted only to authorized personnel. Access rights will be reviewed regularly to ensure compliance with this policy.

## Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party users who have access to organizational data and systems.

## Roles and Responsibilities
- **Management**: Ensure that this policy is enforced and promote awareness within the organization.
- **IT Department**: Implement technical safeguards and conduct regular audits of access rights.
- **Employees**: Comply with the access control policies and report any access-related issues.

## Access Control Measures
1. **User Identification and Authentication**: All users must be uniquely identified and authenticated before being granted access.
2. **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)**: Access rights are assigned based on the user's role within the organization.
3. **Regular Access Reviews**: Access control lists will be reviewed on a quarterly basis to ensure appropriateness of access rights.
4. **Segregation of Duties**: Critical functions should be divided among different personnel to reduce the risk of error or fraud.

## Least Privilege Principle
- Users will be granted the minimum level of access necessary for them to perform their duties. 
- Elevated access rights, such as administrative privileges, will be granted only when absolutely necessary and will be monitored closely.

## Enforcement
Violations of this policy will result in disciplinary action, which may include termination of employment or contract.

## Review and Update
This policy will be reviewed annually and updated as necessary to ensure continued compliance with relevant regulations and standards.

## Conclusion
Adhering to the principle of least privilege protects sensitive information and reduces the attack surface of the organization.
